Why do AI projects often fail?

90% of AI projects fail, not because of technology, but because of wrong scenario selection.

From 200+ projects, our lessons learned boil down to three points:

1. Wrong Scenario
Using AI for the sake of using AI, without clearly defining what business problem to solve.

2. Expectations Too High
Thinking AI can solve everything, only to find AI isn't omnipotent.

3. No One to Implement
Bought the tool and called it done - no one knows how to use it, no one wants to use it.

How to avoid? Right scenario, small steps, get support.

Tool Selection

Coze or Dify - which is better?

Neither is "better" - only "more suitable for you."

Coze: By ByteDance, quick to start, good for rapidly building conversational applications

Dify: Open source, flexible, good for enterprises needing customization

Choice depends on your scenario:

  • Quickly build a chatbot → Recommend Coze
  • Need private deployment → Recommend Dify
  • Integrate with ByteDance ecosystem → Recommend Coze
  • High data security requirements → Recommend Dify
Don't obsess over tools - first think about what problem to solve. Tools can be changed; wrong direction is the real trouble.

When do you need to build your own AI platform?

Most enterprises don't need to build their own - using existing platforms well is enough.

When you need to build your own:

  • Core business heavily depends on AI, need complete control
  • Existing platforms can't meet special requirements
  • Have sufficient technical team and budget
  • AI is the company's core competitive advantage

When you don't need to build your own:

  • Just want to improve efficiency, reduce costs
  • Technical team size is limited
  • Want to quickly validate AI value
We've built our own platform, so we clearly know when to build and when to use existing solutions.

How to judge if an AI project is worth doing?

Calculate ROI clearly - how much saved or earned.

Judgment criteria:

  • Clear Scenario: What specific problem to solve?
  • Sufficient Data: Is there enough data to support it?
  • Calculable ROI: Invest $30K, how much saved/earned?
  • Someone to Implement: Who will use it? How?

If you can't answer these four questions clearly, suggest not doing it yet.
